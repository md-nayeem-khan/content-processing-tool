\subsection{Key requirements}\label{EK9w-eG7VSPiEUqQeEd2p}
\\
The following are the main requirements for the system:

\begin{enumerate}
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{2aSmOWQ-AbWJ0VMOEgC3-}
The system must support a shoe containing one or more standard decks, each consisting of 52 cards across four suits (Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs, Spades) and 13 ranks (Ace through King).
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{MGzvNKrAN_wKGwoIjzH_w}
Cards must have specific point values: numbered cards equal their face value, face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points, and Aces are valued at 1 or 11, depending on the hand's context.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{BtOzKGeOo1AAo4lPKa9EP}
The system includes two user roles: Player and Dealer.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{jLuGcn7sa3HNMpcbJsUJ2}
Players must place a bet at the beginning of each round.
\item
At the start of a round, the dealer deals two cards each to the player and themselves; the player's cards are dealt face-up, while the dealer's hand shows one card face-up and one face-down.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{zpKXYzUefKtQDEzHDoXzH}
Players may choose to \emph{hit} (draw an additional card) as long as their hand total remains under 21, or \emph{stand} to end their turn.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{K_dhcLH_GeXxgJ-4xGn5Z}
The dealer must draw additional cards (hit) until reaching a minimum hand value of 17.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{mzLqA0j17x28tYj8KPEi4}
A hand total exceeding 21 results in a bust and an immediate loss.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{bEpkJ-DOK5GNlOhb0pPy2}
At the end of a round:

\begin{enumerate}
\item
A player wins if their hand total is higher than the dealer's without exceeding 21.
\item
A Blackjack (an Ace and a 10-point card as the initial hand) results in a 3:2 payout.
\item
A tie (push) returns the original bet to the player or may allow a replay.
\end{enumerate}
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{5GgSUs1PEhu2qvpUlZ-y0}
The dealer wins by default if a player leaves or disconnects before a round ends.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Design approach}\label{iHVbgiyqcMA89bPRikDAD}
\\
The Blackjack game was designed using a bottom-up approach, starting with low-level entities (e.g., \texttt{Card}, \texttt{Deck}, \texttt{Hand}) and progressively integrating them into higher-level components such as the \texttt{BlackjackGame}.

\subsection{Design principles}\label{nDx3wq8qPY_5MH-2MQxkr}
\\
Here's how each of the SOLID principles is applied:

\begin{itemize}
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{4NYbgu_RF-vEebmGwvzJ2}
\textbf{Single Responsibility principle (SRP):} Each class has one well-defined responsibility. For example, a \texttt{Card} calculates its value, while \texttt{BlackjackGame} manages game flow.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{brMSYkAnEBcWA0vSmMR0b}
\textbf{Open/Closed principle (OCP):} Classes like \texttt{Player} can be extended via \texttt{BlackjackPlayer} and \texttt{Dealer} without modifying the base.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{dgGkvzalMduslEJV297oH}
\textbf{Liskov Substitution principle (LSP):} \texttt{BlackjackPlayer} and \texttt{Dealer} can be used wherever \texttt{Player} is expected.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{rmkk65rNptMV0lKprLV95}
\textbf{Interface Segregation principle (ISP):} Though not explicitly named, responsibilities are segregated across the view, controller, and model layers.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{LKQkXsvPsJq2JIvaUD-T4}
\textbf{Dependency Inversion principle (DIP):} High-level modules like \texttt{BlackjackGame} depend on abstractions (e.g., \texttt{Player}) rather than concrete implementations.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Design patterns}\label{NPdB6wb4ABdpeEeTFv-sr}
\\
The system applies the following design patterns:

\begin{itemize}
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{VHy3i59cReWrQ_V9Yuc9E}
\textbf{Iterator:} Used to sequentially deal cards from a \texttt{Deck} or \texttt{Shoe} without exposing internal structure.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{fqL6-uu8ogFgZxml3AhEV}
\textbf{State:} Models different game stages (e.g., shuffling, dealing, player's turn, dealer's turn, settlement, game over) as distinct, encapsulated states for clarity and extensibility.
\end{itemize}

This section describes how system objects collaborate to implement key gameplay functionality:

\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{eQ8576tjiV_uqscwqckBd}

\textbf{Card dealing:} \texttt{BlackjackGame} draws cards from the \texttt{Shoe} and assigns them to the \texttt{Hand} objects of all players and the dealer at the start of the round.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{tJJxJd1B6gkOP3egGA_v6}
\textbf{Player turn:} \texttt{BlackjackController} validates each player's chosen action (hit or stand) and updates their \texttt{Hand} accordingly by drawing cards.
\item
\protect\phantomsection\label{hLe_0ycgp3AlWnzuy6X4D}
\textbf{Dealer turn:} The \texttt{Dealer} reveals the hidden card and draws additional cards as needed until reaching a total hand value of at least 17.

\textbf{Outcome evaluation:} \texttt{BlackjackGame} evaluates all hands, compares them against the dealer's, and determines the result for each player (win, loss, push).

\protect\phantomsection\label{cTFI_jmcPv9m-Dvbi5UJi}

\subsection{System workflow}\label{9uHvpIa24EmyvQwr2OrwG}
\\
This section outlines the primary end-to-end scenarios in the system:

\begin{itemize}
\item
\textbf{Creating or logging into an account:} A player or dealer begins by registering a new account or logging into an existing one. The system verifies credentials, activates the session, and provides access to game-related features.
\item
\textbf{Setting up and joining a game:} The dealer creates a new Blackjack table, making it visible in the list of open games. Players browse the list and join an available game. Once the minimum number of players is reached, the game becomes ready to start.
\item
\textbf{Placing bets:} At the start of each round, all active players are prompted to place their bets. The system validates each wager against the player's available balance and stores the bet amounts for payout calculation.
\item
\textbf{Dealing initial cards:} The system draws cards from the shoe and deals two to each player and the dealer. Players receive both cards face up, while the dealer's hand shows one face up and one face down.
\item
\textbf{Player turn:} Each player takes their turn in sequence. They can hit (draw a card) or stand (end their turn). If a player hits, the system adds a card to their hand and checks for a bust. The turn ends when the player stands or busts.
\item
\textbf{Dealer turn:} After all players have acted, the dealer reveals the hidden card. Following house rules, the dealer draws cards until the hand total reaches at least 17 or a bust occurs.
\item
\textbf{Settlement and payout:} The system evaluates each player's outcomes once all hands are finalized. It determines wins, losses, pushes, or Blackjacks. The system calculates payouts or collects the losing bets based on the result.
\item
\textbf{Round conclusion and continuation:} The round results are displayed to all players through the game view. The system resets necessary states and prepares for the next round, allowing players to continue or leave.
\end{itemize}
